How Solar Panels Work

Understanding the science behind solar energy conversion

Sunlight Absorption

Solar cells absorb photons from sunlight, creating electron-hole pairs in the semiconductor material.

Electricity Generation

The movement of these electrons creates direct current (DC) electricity within the solar cells.

Current Conversion

An inverter converts the DC electricity into alternating current (AC) for home or business use.

Energy Distribution

The converted electricity powers your appliances or feeds excess back into the grid.

Solar Panel Maintenance

Proper maintenance ensures optimal performance and longevity of your solar energy system:

  • Regular Cleaning

    Clean panels every 1-3 months to remove dust, dirt, and debris that can reduce efficiency by up to 25%.

  • Shade Monitoring

    Regularly check for new obstructions like growing trees that may cast shadows on your panels.

  • Damage Inspection

    Conduct visual inspections for cracks, loose connections, or other physical damage to the system.

  • Performance Tracking

    Monitor energy output regularly to detect any significant drops in performance that may indicate issues.

  • Professional Checkups

    Schedule annual professional inspections to ensure all components are functioning optimally.
